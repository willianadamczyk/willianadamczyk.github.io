PhD in Economics & Data Scientist

Combining economic intuition with advanced data science and evidence-based policy evaluation to analyze labour markets, the future of work, and lifelong learning.

Profile overview

Willian Boschetti Adamczyk is an economist and data scientist, with experience in labour economics, public policy and applied data science. From 2022 to 2026, he worked at the International Labour Organization (ILO, Geneva), contributing to the ILO flagship report Lifelong Learning and Skills for the Future. He holds a PhD in development economics from the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio Grande do Sul (PUCRS) and is currently a postdoctoral researcher in management at Univali, as well as a visiting researcher at the Instituto de Pesquisa Econômica Aplicada (IPEA, Rio de Janeiro). His research applies data science, machine learning and natural language processing, using R, Python and Spark, to analyse labour markets and policy interventions. Previously, he served as research coordinator at the National School of Public Administration (ENAP), delivering rapid, evidence-based insights to inform policymaking and supervising policy reports for the Brazilian Federal Government in areas including education, labour, disability and culture.

Research focus

Future of work

investigating how technological advancements, particularly AI and automation, reshape occupational structures and demand lifelong learning.

Skills and lifelong learning

researching skill demands, mismatches, and the importance of lifelong learning for modern workforce development.

Green employment

analyzing the labor market implications of the green transition and identifying the skills required for the sustainable economy.

Policy evaluation

applying rigorous quasi-experimental designs and econometric methodologies to assess the causal impact of public policies and interventions.
